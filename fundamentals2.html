Operators in JavaScript are symbols or keywords that perform operations on one or more operands. These operations can include arithmetic calculations, logical evaluations, assignment, comparison, and more. Operators are a fundamental part of any programming language, including JavaScript, and they play a crucial role in expressing and manipulating data within your code.

  • Arithmetic Operators
  • Assignment Operators
  • Comparison Operators
  • Logical Operators

Ans 2:

Arithmetic Operators:Arithmetic operators perform mathematical calculations on numeric values.

Assignment Operators:Assignment operators assign values to variables.

Comparison Operators:Comparison operators compare values and return a Boolean result.

Logical Operators:Logical operators perform logical operations and return a Boolean result.

Increment and Decrement Operators:Increment (++) and decrement (--) operators increase or decrease the value of a variable by 1.

IntroductionIn JavaScript, operators are categorized based on the number of operands they act upon. The three main categories are unary, binary, and ternary operators.

Unary Operators:Unary operators are operators that perform an operation on a single operand. They require only one value to operate upon.

Binary Operators:Binary operators are operators that perform an operation on two operands. They require two values to operate upon.

Ternary Operator:The ternary operator is a unique operator in JavaScript that takes three operands. It is often used as a shorthand for an if-else statement.
